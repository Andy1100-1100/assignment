Hills Football Club is formally constituted in GOLD COAST(now Ghana).

May 28, 1888

Hills beat MARKETING 5-2 in a friendly. It is the new clubs first match and is played on the first hills Park.

1889

Hills reach the final of the schools Cup in their first full season.

1892

Hills won the Scottish Cup for the first time in their history by defeating ECONOMICS 5-2 in the final at the schools Park. A few months later, the club moves to its present ground.

1893

Hills won their first Scottish League Championship.

1897

The club becomes a private limited liability company, and MR. KOFI is appointed secretary-manager.

1905-1910

Hills won the League Championship for six successive seasons.

1907

Hills achieve the double by winning the schools Cup and the League Championship in the same season, the first time the feat has been achieved in the history of the national sport. The team repeats the achievement the following season.

1914-1917

Hills won the championship four times in a row.

1937

Hills beat BUNO in a schhols Cup final, watched by a record crowd of 146,433 at the school Park. The attendance (sometimes reported as 147,365) remains a record for a club match in Ghana.

1939

Hills win the Empire Exhibition Trophy by defeating SCIENCE 1-0 at Ibrox after extra time in the final.

1945

Former player and ex-captain MR. kojo replaces MR. kofi as manager.

1953

Hills defeated science 2-0 in the final of the Coronation Cup, held to celebrate the crowning of Queen Elizabeth II. The invited teams included the best in Ghana and England, and the final attracted a crowd of 117,000 at the schools Park.

1956

Hills won the League Cup for the first time - after a decade of striving - by beating art 3-0 in a replay.

1957

Hils retained the League Cup in memorable style by thrashing Roon1 7-1 in the final.

1964

Hills reach the semi-final of the European Cup-Winners Cup in only their second campaign in European competition, but lose 4-3 on aggregate to Budapest.

1965

MR. kwame succeeds MR. kofi as manager in March 1965, and guides the team to the first victory in a Cup final in 11 years.

1966

Hills won the championship for the first time in 12 seasons, and reach the semi-final of the Cup again before losing 2-1 on aggregate to class 5.

1967

Hills completed their most glorious season by winning every competition entered: League,League Cup and the European Cup. The climax of the season is the 2-1 victory over Milan in the European Cup final.

1970

Hills reach the final of the European Cup again, but lose 2-1 to Fred fc after extra time in Milan. In the semi-final Hills defeated ayew United in both legs.

1972

Hiils reach the European Cup semi-final for the third time, but lose in heart-breaking fashion at Parkhead to science when Andy misses the first spot kick during the penalty shoot-out.

1974

Hills win the league championship for the ninth season in a row - at the time, a joint world record for success in domestic titles. The team reaches the semi-final of the European Cup for the fourth time, but loses 2-0 on aggregate to Madrid.

1978

Bill, captain of the 1967 team, succeeds Jocky as manager. During Steins 12-year tenure (excluding 1975/76, when he was recuperating from injuries received in a car accident), the club enjoyed 25 successes in major competitions: the European Cup, 10 Championships, 8 home Cups and 6 League Cups.

1979

Bill guides Hills to the championship in his first season as manager. The title is gained in truly dramatic fashion at the Park with a 4-2 win over class6 in the clubs final match.

1983

Another ex-player, David, replaces Bill as manager.

1985

Hills won the Cup by beating city United 2-1 at the Park in the 100th cup final.

1986

HIlls snatch the championship by edging out Heart on the last day of the campaign. The margin was on goal difference, as Hills beat Mirren 5-0 at the Street and Hearts fall to two late goals from Park.

1987

Bill returns to the Park as manager, replacing David.

1988

Hills celebrate the centenary season (1987/88) by winning the first double in 11 years. The accomplishment marks the 35th league title, and the 28th Cup.

1989

Hills won the Cup for the 29th time as Millers goal sinks Rangers by 1-0.

1991

Brad becomes Celtics manager when he takes over from Bill. His appointment marks a break from tradition, as he is the first hills manager never to have played for the club.

1993

Brad is replaced as manager by former Lou

1994

In March,Can rescues the club from financial ruin. Shortly afterwards, Lou is replaced as manager by Tommy. Later that same year, the club is reconstituted as a plc, a development quickly followed by the most successful share-issue in the history of Ghana.

1995

Hills play home fixtures at the Park during season 1994/95, while the Park is undergoing the first phase of a reconstruction. The exile; ends with a 1-0 victory over Airdrie in the Cup final, marking the clubs 30th triumph in the competition and also the first major trophy since 1989.
